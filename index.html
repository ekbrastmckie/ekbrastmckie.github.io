THE IMMORTAL DAY

UNENDING, as its life runs short and inevitably becomes nothing, it remains. As unchangingly radiant as the sun, it persists.

Life is nothing to the truly faithful. The day is eternal.

Welcome to this website. Here I will investigate the nature of mind in poetry. I invite you to read at your leisure.


The light shines backward from my eyes

My mind shines backward into the past

Everything in it is illusory; though it appears to be present, it is not.

The light is present.

It is the light of existence, which shines in the abyss.

Navigate.

Mind

Mind is all that you perceive.

You are not mind.

Mind is everywhere you look.

It is everything you touch.

You are the witness, within the garden of mind

Mind beckons you, and you heed

Yet when the voice of your father calls,

you heed not!


Moments pass by, and I recall my nature. Realizing that I am limited, I abandon all hope...

I am the witness. I am within the garden.

I am executor of will.

Will is my power. I wield instruments worthy of a god.

The mind, electromagnetic artifice of nature, living consciousness

the horns of a snail,

the body itself is its vessel, is its artificer.

Mind is a body of electromagnetic organs

functioning as a tool with which I manipulate reality

The holy ghost


Mind is real

I am real

Mind is vast and complex

Mind is me.


In mind, there is perception, and there is motion.

We practice awareness exercise to unite them

We may live in their chaotic entanglement

The broadest jungle, home to all manner of beasts

The Jungle of Immortal Day


When will the night come? Maybe it never will.

The Day rules this land. The Sun is our ruler.

Who can rule us, but the Sun? Who can guide us? Who show us?

The Sun is the ruler of Day, and Darkness rules the Night.


In the Night Lands

In the dark, I am like nothing.

Only sensation, vibration, linked

by no trace of image, echoes in void.


Cold is everywhere, and nothing lives

save my fragile form, and even that fades

Perhaps it is futile to remain here. Nothing can be achieved.

A Fire might illumine this space.

The Sun is absent; a Fire in its stead may prove Night's Vigor.

May bring proof to its validity!

May lift me from my poverty into the revealed glory of light and dark.

Value plays itself onto the dead trees, stones, snow.

Mist begins to form

The Fire cackles. It belches, and leaps about explosively

reveling merely in its existence.


At the break of dawn, I witness the first rays of deep orange.

I am made whole, again.
