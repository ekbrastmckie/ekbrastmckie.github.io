THE NIGHT


Welcome to the night.

Herein, my humble abode lying in shadow, //STOP LYING//
I waken. I am humble!
Herein do I sleep. I am humble!

The night is dark...
In the night we sleep.
Lit by the moon
How could I but wake?
awake, perhaps, too soon.

Rely upon the sun, and night shall be thy undoing.
Rely upon the dark, and the sun shall destroy thee.
Trust in thyself, in thyself

Sleeping the sleep of the damned
I awaken to darkness, and
I thinking my eyes had been blind
proceeded until I did find
the Moon with her shimmering glow
relieved me of fear. Even so,
I shivered and drew myself close.
What was this strange, dim and shady, dark?
What is this sense of foreboding?
Only paranoia? I am afraid of the dark

The night is dark.
Blessed is the dark, which covers up our eyes that we might be made innocent once more.

The night is dark.
Blessed is the dark, which hides us from those who would destroy us.

The night is dark.
Blessed is the dark, blessed the night. Protect us from harm, hold us and cleanse us
remove from us our burdens
remove from us our senses

Night is our mother, and sleep is her womb,
from which each morning we emerge.
Sleep is like death, and Night is our tomb,
to which each evening we return.


Blessed are you, Night,
and may you return ever at the end of Day.

Dawn is your death and dusk your birth, o Night
May it ever be so.

Birth, and Death
Good, and Bad
Day, and Night
Mind, and the Real.
All of these are required, for the sake of balance.
Without Night, Day is an Immortal Oppressor.
Without Day, Night is a Lifeless Winter.
Without Bad, Good leads to rage.
Without Good, Bad leads to despair.
Without Mind, the Real is unknown.
Without the Real, Mind is empty
of meaning,
among other things.
